Kibi

Description

This is a lebanese recipe based in the Quebbe. It arrived to the yucatan peninsula from the lebase inmigration from the early 20th century. Now, it has been adopted as part of the local gastronomy of the region.

There are different variations around the peninsula depending of the preference in the region, ranging from the tradition ball of meat to sandwich. Even with all their differences, they share the same basic ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kg of beef mince meat
  • 1 onion
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 chiles serranos
  • 1/2 kg of bulgur wheat(fine)
  • 1 egg
  • 10 peppermint branches
  • 1 tablespoon of chicken stock powder
  • Pinch of pepper
  • 1 bitter orange
  • Salt
  • Vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Finely chop the onion, peppermint, tomato, garlic and chiles. Soak the wheat until it is soft.
  2. Once the wheat is done drain it completely until you have removed almost all huminity.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the meat, onion, peppermint, garlic, tomato and knead. Afterwards, add the egg, salt and pepper.
  4. Add the chicken stock and wheat to the meat and keep mixing until all ingredients are well blended
  5. Make small meat balls and shape them as a small diamond. Deep fry them until golden.
  6. Drain any leftover oil from the fried diamonds and remove any excess with a napkin. Serve with sliced of bitter orange.
